This next step in the study involves providing information from Safari about how you use web search engines and how you use the web overall. This information will be based on Safari's browsing history, and it will include when and how you ran searches as well as counts of how many websites and webpages you visited over the past 30 days.

Your privacy and data security are of the utmost importance to us. We have implemented technical safeguards to prevent us from collecting your search queries, the other websites or webpages that you have visited, or browser information that identifies you. You will have an opportunity to preview your information before you provide it. You may also review a detailed description of the types of data that we will collect here.

Please continue for step-by-step directions on how to provide this information from Safari.

Please follow the steps below to provide web search and browsing information for this study.

  1. Go to Settings > Apps > Safari.

  2. Below History and Website Data, tap Export.

  3. Select only History to export.
    If a Profile option appears, select All.

  4. Tap Save to Downloads. Your iPhone may require Face ID, Touch ID, or a password.

  5. Tap Choose File below, then select the Safari Export [date].zip file that downloaded on your iPhone. You should be able to locate this file by tapping Browse twice, then iCloud Drive or On My iPhone (depending on which appeared earlier), then Downloads. If you have difficulty locating this file, we recommend searching for Safari Export in iCloud Drive and On My iPhone.

    This webpage will automatically check for the correct file and filter out unnecessary information. If you would like to see the exact data that we will receive from your web browser, it will appear in the text box below before you submit it to us.
